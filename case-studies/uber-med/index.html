The Problem

For 5.8 millions of Americans, transportation is a major barrier to accessing healthcare.

The Solution

How might we enable Uber patients with physical and cognitive barriers to reach their healthcare appointments on time with personalized support?

My concept: Uber Med introduces three core changes to the existing Uber Health experience — personalized ride preparation, proactive appointment reminders tied to transit time, and clearer handoff between clinic and driver.

Research

I interviewed patients who rely on non-emergency medical transport, reviewed NEMT complaint data, and mapped the full journey from appointment booking to clinic arrival. Key insight: anxiety spikes in the 24 hours before an appointment when ride details are unclear.

Research synthesis

Process

I prototyped three flows in Figma — ride preparation, live trip coordination, and post-visit feedback — and tested them with five participants who use mobility aids or caregivers. Iterations focused on reducing cognitive load during high-stress moments.

Process artifacts

Takeaways

Healthcare transportation is not just a logistics problem — it is an equity problem. Designing for patients with barriers means building confidence into every touchpoint, not bolting accessibility on at the end.
